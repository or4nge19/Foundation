# Aristotle Glossary

## Core Terms

- `Lexis`: the linguistic item as spoken or written.
- `Term`: the simple expression used in categorial and syllogistic contexts.
- `Signified`: the thing picked out by a term.
- `Logos`: the account under which a term signifies a thing.
- `Expression`: either a simple `Term` or a combined expression.
- `DefinitionalPhrase`: a structured genus-plus-differentia phrase kept distinct
  from a bare combined term.
- `DefinitionKind`: the explicit split between dialectical and scientific
  definition.
- `DialecticalDefinition`: a `DefinitionalPhrase` taken under dialectical use,
  as a candidate account to be screened and tested.
- `ScientificDefinition`: a `DefinitionalPhrase` taken under scientific use, as
  a definition belonging to explanatory science.
- Dialectical definition: a genus-plus-differentia proposal that survives the
  Topics-style tests needed to orient inquiry, without yet becoming a causal
  account in a science.
- Scientific definition: a stricter account whose terms are fixed by
  demonstration and explanatory cause, not merely by dialectical survival.

## De Interpretatione Layer

- `TermOccurrence`: a term as it occurs inside an assertion, either simple,
  negated, or many-as-one.
- `Assertion`: the current assertoric statement object, carrying subject,
  predicate, quantity, quality, and temporal status before the later
  syllogistic restriction.
- `AssertionQuantity`: the current four-way split between singular, universal,
  particular, and indefinite assertion.
- `TemporalStatus`: the current coarse distinction between nonfuture, settled
  future, and contingent future assertion.
- `RCPException`: the current typed list of ways an assertion can fall outside
  ordinary contradictory distribution: indefinite, many-as-one subject,
  many-as-one predicate, and singular future contingent.
- `IsRCPRegular`: the current marker for whether an assertion falls under the
  ordinary contradictory distribution.
- `Categorical`: the universal/particular assertoric fragment extracted from the
  broader assertion layer for later syllogistic use.

## Categories Layer

- `CategorialCandidate`: a term together with the referent and logos fixed by a
  univocal use.
- `PositionedCandidate`: a `CategorialCandidate` together with a unique
  categorial placement.
- `GenusCompatibility`: same-category agreement between two positioned
  candidates.
- `ProvisionalGenus`: a genus proposal that has survived screening, category
  placement, and the current genus-side positive witness check.
- `RecognizedSubstance`: the current umbrella API for substance recognition; it
  is being refined into explicit primary and secondary substance criteria.
- Primary substance: what is neither said of a subject nor in a subject, and
  carries the this-something criterion.
- Secondary substance: what is said of a subject but not in a subject, without
  thereby inheriting primary thisness.
- `thesisRefutationTargets`: the list of contradictory commitments against which
  a staged dialectical thesis can be tested in `Elenchus`.

## Topics Terms

- `endoxon`: what seems acceptable to everyone, most, or the wise, taken in the
  dialectical setting as a proposition available for questioning and concession.
- `dialectical premiss`: an acceptable proposition asked in the right
  yes-or-no form for dialectical use.
- `dialectical problem`: a disputed proposition worth examining for choice,
  avoidance, or truth.
- `thesis`: a special dialectical problem, usually counter-opinional or backed
  by an argument against prevailing opinion.
- `topos` or `location`: a recipe for finding premisses from a desired
  conclusion.
- `induction`: proceeding from similar or particular cases to a universal.
- `arrangement`: the questioner-side ordering and presentation of premisses in
  an exchange.
- `objection`: the answerer-side identification of an exception, ambiguity, or
  other blockage against a universal move.

## Prior and Posterior Analytics

- `PredicationChain`: an acyclic semantic chain of terms.
- `TermChain`: proof-search bookkeeping for inserted middle terms.
- `Science`: a subject matter plus an admissibility criterion for propositions.
- `ImmediateIn`: the current science-relative notion of immediacy; it is being
  strengthened toward an explicitly middle-sensitive and expansion-sensitive
  notion. The A-case is now carried by a dedicated `ImmediateAIn`.
- `ImmediateAIn`: the current explicit A-proposition form of immediacy, blocking
  distinct Barbara middles inside a science.
- `Nous`: grasp of a proposition as immediate within a science.
- `ScientificDefinitionIn`: a scientific definition whose defining statements
  function as first principles inside a given science.
- `MiddleExplainsIn`: the witness that a middle term explains, rather than
  merely validly links, the minor and major in a demonstration.
- `NonReciprocalIn`: the current expansion-sensitive anti-return condition
  blocking a demonstrative conclusion from reaching its premises back again.
- `Demonstration`: a figured syllogism whose premises are first principles in a
  science and whose middle is explanatory; it must not collapse into validity
  alone.

## Metaphysics and Physics

- `BeingProfile`: the D7 profile of a being across accidentality, category, and
  modal status.
- `TruthStatus`: the thought-side status through which truth is located in
  thought rather than treated as one more flat mode of being.
- `trueThought`: the current thought-level carrier for being-as-truth; the
  thought/being interface now allows true thought still to concern separate
  beings.
- `PathToPrinciples`: the Reading-B articulation of confused universal
  descriptions into more particular ones.
- `LocalCausalAlignment`: a local coincidence witness tying efficient or final
  causes to a formal cause without imposing a global reduction.
